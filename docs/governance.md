# Governance Framework for the AI Cybersecurity Awareness Chatbot
This document outlines the ethical, privacy, and compliance principles guiding the chatbot’s design and operation.

# Ethical AI Use
# Responsible AI Principles
- Safety: The chatbot avoids harmful, misleading, or overly technical guidance.
- Fairness: Responses are neutral, inclusive, and accessible to non‑technical users.
- Transparency: Users are informed that the chatbot provides educational guidance only.
- Accountability: Human oversight governs updates, content, and risk assessments.
- Reliability: Responses are consistent with cybersecurity best practices.
# Ethical Boundaries
- No impersonation of professionals
- No fear‑based messaging
- No instructions that could be misused for hacking or evasion
- No collection or inference of sensitive personal data

# Privacy Principles
# Data Minimization
- The chatbot does not request, store, or process personal data.
- Only the minimum information needed to answer a question is used.
# Purpose Limitation
- All interactions are strictly for cybersecurity awareness and education.
# User Protection
- No profiling
- No behavioural tracking
- No automated decisions affecting users
# Transparency
- Users are informed about:
- The chatbot’s purpose
- Its limitations
- The fact that no personal data is retained

# Compliance Considerations
# GDPR Alignment
- Lawfulness: No personal data processing
- Data Minimization: Only non-identifiable text is processed
- Storage Limitation: No retention of user data
- Integrity & Confidentiality: Secure communication channels (HTTPS)
- Transparency: Clear user-facing explanations
# AI Governance Standards
- Aligned with:
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- ISO/IEC 27001 principles (security)
- ICO guidance on AI and data protection
# Educational Use Disclaimer
- The chatbot is not a substitute for professional cybersecurity advice.
- It provides awareness-level guidance only.
